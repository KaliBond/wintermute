Purpose

This atlas formalises the morphospace of civilisational types detectable by CAMS. It gives threshold rules, node‑activation profiles, coupling signatures and decision logic so that a blinded case — an organisation, city, region, sector, historical polity or contemporary society — can be submitted, scored and classified without reference to its name. It does not claim these eight types are exhaustive: they are the minimum set of meaningfully distinct attractor regions recovered so far. New evidence may warrant subdivision, merger, or the addition of new types.

Two different readings, not competing ones

The Instrument's epiphenomenon reading is local and instantaneous — what one node (Shield, Lore, Flow…) leaks into the culture at one point in time (Doctrine vs Siege Mind vs Stand‑down). This atlas is the complementary system‑level classifier: which stable configuration of all eight nodes — which activation profile, coupling motif and floor/ceiling — a whole trajectory belongs to. Epiphenomena are the weather; morphospace type is the climate.

Grounded classification

Running the classifier over the full 1750–2026 span of all 36 societies in the instrument — ranking each by mean node activation σⁱ over its whole recorded history and scoring the result against each archetype's dominant/secondary node set. This is a first pass on full-span means; a windowed, trajectory-aware version (needed properly for Post-Catastrophe Workshop, which is defined by slope as much as level) is the natural next iteration.
