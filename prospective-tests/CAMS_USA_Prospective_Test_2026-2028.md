**Severity weight:** HIGH — directly tests attractor class identification.

### Prediction P2: Executive Decoupling Epiphenomenon — Observable Policy Incoherence

1. Major legislative or executive agenda reversal within 12 months after announcement

**Severity weight:** MEDIUM — tests epiphenomenon prediction accuracy.

## 6. Interpretive Notes for Adjudicators

### On "failure" vs "revision"
A prediction fail does not falsify CAMS. It triggers the specific revision protocol implied by the failed criterion. If P1 fails (B recovers > 13.0), it means the Re-synchronisation attractor was active and the model's attractor classification was incorrect for this period — requiring revision of the Fracture classification protocol, not the framework's core thermodynamic claims.[^1]

Only a pattern of > 4 failures across the 7 predictions would trigger FC6 and require fundamental revision of the dynamical model.[^1]

### On external shocks
The CAMS forward model includes an external forcing term F_t (policy shocks, exogenous events). A large-scale shock (war, pandemic, economic collapse) that materially alters node states after April 2026 is not a failure of the model — it is an input to the model. Adjudicators should note any such events and assess whether the model's *response dynamics* (rate of B recovery or decline following the shock) match CAMS predictions, rather than treating the shock itself as a failure condition.[^1]

### On observable versus quantitative predictions
Predictions P2 and P3 rely on observable public-record events rather than ensemble scores. Their adjudication is intentionally conservative: the pass criteria require *multiple independent indicators*, not single anecdotal events, to reduce the risk of false confirmation.

Whether that trajectory resolves, reverses, or accelerates is what the 2026–2028 window will determine.[^3]
