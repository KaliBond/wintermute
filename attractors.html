CAMS System Attractors

Civilizational Archetypes in Complex Adaptive Systems

🎭 Six Fundamental Attractors

🚀 Optimization Engine

Ψ ≥ 0.60, Low Stress, High Abstraction
Peak-performing societies with sophisticated learning systems. Examples: Singapore (1990-2020), Denmark (2000-2020)

📈 Steady Climber

Ψ ≥ 0.60, Low Stress, Moderate Abstraction
Reliable, consistent growth with institutional stability. Examples: Canada (1950-2020), Australia (1980-2010)

🔥 Phoenix Transformer

High Asymmetry, Rebuilding Phase
Post-crisis transformation with innovation potential. Examples: Germany (1945-1970), Japan (1945-1980), South Korea (1960-1990)

🔄 Resilient Innovator

Cycling Moderate Stress, Dynamic Learning
Beneficial stress-driven adaptation. Examples: USA (1930-1980), UK (1940-1980), Israel (1948-1990)

🏛️ Stable Core

Ψ 0.35-0.50, Status Quo Maintenance
Strong institutions, limited adaptability. Examples: France (1960-1990), Italy (1970-2000)

⚠️ Fragile High-Stress

Ψ < 0.35, High Stress, Crisis Vulnerability
Destructive stress requiring intervention. Examples: Weimar Germany (1930-1933), Soviet Union (1985-1991)

🔄 Transition Dynamics

Societies transition between attractors through natural evolution, crisis-driven transformation, policy-induced changes, or stress-induced degradation.
