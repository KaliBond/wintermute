The foundational insight is that human societies are Complex Adaptive Systems and their CAS dynamics can be recovered from history in human archives.

From this insight the eight nodes and their metrics were developed. CAMS is the resulting measurement system. It tracks security, knowledge systems, material conditions, strategic direction, memory, execution, resource stewardship, and circulation — and how their interactions evolve over time. When these nodes are well-coupled, societies tend to be resilient. When they decouple, stress accumulates faster than adaptive capacity and coordination fails.

This work is both theoretically grounded and empirically developed. The foundational mapping came from first principles. What followed was systematic recovery of CAS dynamics from historical archives, iterative refinement, and rigorous testing across many societies and eras.
